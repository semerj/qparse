OSLO, Norway (AP) _ {{Alexey Prokurorov}} had no real challengers as {{Alexey Prokurorov}} won the 50-kilometer classical-style cross country World Cup race in Holmenkollen Saturday, clocking 2 hours, 32 minutes, 25.3 seconds. Four Norwegians chased behind, but Odd Bjorn Hjelmeseth, {{Bjorn Dahlie}}, Anders Aukland and Erling Jevne had to settle with second to fifth places. {{Alexey Prokurorov}} was 1:18.9 ahead of Hjelmeseth. {{Thomas Alsgaard}} clinched the World Cup Wednesday in Sweden and {{Thomas Alsgaard}} had nothing to go for in the 50K, finishing 20th. {{Thomas Alsgaard}} had 801 points in the final overall standings. {{Bjorn Dahlie}}, who became the winningest Winter Olympian in history at Nagano last month, finished second with 678 points. No other skier has more World Cup race wins than {{Bjorn Dahlie}}, {{Bjorn Dahlie}}. {{Larissa Lazutina}} won the women's overall title comfortably when {{Larissa Lazutina}} defeated teammate Svetlana Nageykina by 2:23.9 in the 30K classical-style race. {{Larissa Lazutina}} finished in 1:41.41.2. {{Larissa Lazutina}} topped the final standings with 773 points. Anita Moen-Guidon of Norway was third, 2:28.6 behind {{Larissa Lazutina}}, and Russia's Julia Chepalova fourth, 2:53.5 behind.
