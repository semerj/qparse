KRASNOYARSK, Russia (AP) _ The candidates for governor of the Krasnoyarsk region of Siberia have employed all the tools available to modern politics: polling, television advertising _ and, apparently, dirty tricks. The election, set for Sunday, has attracted national attention because Russia's former security chief {{Alexander Lebed}} is one of the candidates. Russian news agencies reported Friday that police had confiscated 850,000 copies of a newspaper published by {{Alexander Lebed}} because it allegedly contained libelous material about {{Valery Zubov}}, {{Valery Zubov}}. {{Alexander Lebed}} called the charge "absurd," and said unspecified rivals had been carrying out dirty tricks against {{Alexander Lebed}}, including having someone hand out condoms to young schoolchildren in {{Alexander Lebed}} name. The Lebed newspaper, Krasnoyarski Krai, was confiscated on Wednesday from a printing plant, authorities said. Local television showed copies of the newspaper and the offending article, headlined "Look out, Provocation!" The article declared that {{Valery Zubov}} was considering acts of vandalism, including "accidents at the power stations, the cutting off of electricity, water, transportation, communications... to provoke dissatisfaction of the populace and to cause panic." {{Yuri Vasliev}}, {{Yuri Vasliev}}, declined to comment on the article Friday, except to say that the matter needed to be settled in court. {{Alexander Lebed}}, in an interview with The Associated Press, denied any knowledge of the article and said there had been no justification for seizing the newspapers. "{{Alexander Lebed}} think it is the activity of the governor," {{Alexander Lebed}} said. {{Alexander Lebed}} went on to say that there had been numerous dirty tricks played on {{Alexander Lebed}}. "{{Alexander Lebed}} am categorically against these dirty tricks," {{Alexander Lebed}} said. "{{Alexander Lebed}} am eager to increase positive things." (ml/gm)
